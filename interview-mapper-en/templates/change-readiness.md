# Lens: Change Readiness (stakeholder)

**When:** interview with a stakeholder/employee BEFORE a specific transformation/reorganization — goal is to map the political landscape around a decision that hasn't been made yet or is being rolled out. Difference from org-mapping-vmdi: that one is a snapshot of the current state of the system as a whole; this one is targeted at ONE upcoming change, the coalitions for/against it, and the hidden interests around it. Difference from expert: that one assesses domain expertise regardless of personal stance; here, stance and personal interest are the main subject.
**Coding unit:** a statement of position on a specific change with a quote + separately — the presumed hidden interest (not what is said formally, but what is lost/gained personally).
**Collection discipline:** keep "what is said publicly" and "what can be inferred about personal interest" explicitly separate (the latter is interpretation, not fact — flag as hypothesis).

## Layer 1 — operational (facts + quote + line)
- **R1 Stated position on the change** — for / against / neutral, in the respondent's own words.
- **R2 Official reason for the position** — what they name as justification (efficiency, risks, organizational values).
- **R3 Who else is for and against, per the respondent** — names/roles named by the respondent, and which side they place them on.
- **R4 Past experience with similar changes** — how similar transformations went in this organization before, per the respondent's memory — success/failure and why.
- **R5 Conditions for support** — what would need to happen for the respondent to move from neutral/opponent to active supporter.
- **R6 Expected personal losses/gains for the respondent** — what the respondent themselves names as a possible personal gain/loss (authority, status, workload, budget).
- **R7 Channel of influence** — through whom/what the respondent usually pushes their position in the organization (meetings, informal conversations, memos).

## Layer 2 — analytical (conclusion + quote; CAPS) — to council
- **A1 Public position vs hidden interest** — where the official justification (R2) may be masking a personal interest (R6) — interpretation, flag as HYPOTHESIS, not fact. *(unstable — latent)*
- **A2 Coalition map** — (assembled after several interviews) clusters "for" / "against" / "wait-and-see", based on overlapping R3 mentions across respondents.
- **A3 Influence strength** — how much the respondent can actually affect the outcome, judging by channel (R7) and role, not just by how loud their position is.
- **A4 Conditional support (R5) — specificity** — a concrete, measurable condition vs a vague deflection ("if it's all done fairly") — a vague condition often signals hidden disagreement that no compromise will resolve.
- **A5 Risk of repeating a past failure** — does the current situation match the pattern of a failed change from R4.
- **A6 Key insight** — 1 sentence: on change [X], the respondent holds position [Y], the likely real interest is [hypothesis Z], influence strength is [high/low], risk is [W].

## Forcing questions
What will personally change for you if this happens? Who in the organization will be happy about this, and who won't, and why? Has something similar happened before — how did it turn out? What would need to happen for you to support this 100%? How do you usually push your position here?

## Scope and ethics
This is the only lens where the interviewer deliberately builds a political map of people — handle A1/A2 with maximum care: hypotheses about hidden interest MUST NOT be shared with respondents without anonymization, and they must not become the sole basis for personnel decisions without independent human verification.
