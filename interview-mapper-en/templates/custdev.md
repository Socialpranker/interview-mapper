# Lens: CustDev / Problem Discovery (The Mom Test)

**When:** problem/discovery interview to validate a need before building a solution. Goal — separate facts from polite lies.
**Coding unit:** a fact about past behavior with a quote. Opinions and compliments — separately and with skepticism.
**Rule:** talk about their life, not your idea; ask about specific past events, not hypotheticals; listen 80% of the time.

## Layer 1 — operational (facts + quote + line)
- **C1 Problem/task** — what problem they describe in their own concrete words; how it manifests.
- **C2 Real episodes** — specific past cases (when, what they did), NOT "usually I…".
- **C3 Current solution / workaround** — how they cover it now, including hacks (Excel, manual work, "I ask a colleague").
- **C4 Cost of the problem** — how much it has already cost: time, money, nerves, missed opportunities. Numbers.
- **C5 Who else is involved** — who suffers/decides/pays; roles in decision-making.
- **C6 What they've tried and dropped** — which solutions they hired and why they gave up.

## Layer 2 — analytical (conclusion + quote; CAPS) — to council
- **A1 Fact vs opinion** — what is confirmed by BEHAVIOR, and what is only a declaration/compliment. Flag explicitly. *(unstable)*
- **A2 Strength of pain** — BURNING / NAGGING / IMAGINED. Marker: whether they already paid/spent a resource. *(unstable)*
- **A3 Readiness signals** — did they search for a solution themselves? spend money? call in others? (real commitment signals, not "would be interesting").
- **A4 Hypothesis: confirmed/refuted** — which hypothesis the interview confirmed/refuted and with what exactly.
- **A5 False-positive risk** — where answers might have been politeness; what should be re-checked.
- **A6 Key insight** — 1 sentence: problem [X] is real/doubtful, because [behavioral evidence], therefore [what to do].

## Forcing questions
When did this last happen? What did you do? What happened next? How much did it cost? What did you try to solve it? Why didn't it work? (Avoid: "would you buy…", "do you like the idea…".)
