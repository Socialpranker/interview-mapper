# Lens: Positioning / Brand / Naming

**When:** interview (expert / employee / visitor) focused on the name, brand, positioning, rebranding.
**Unit:** code. Cell = **quote → What it reveals → → Recommendation**.
**Rule:** distinguish a reaction to the WORD (name) from a reaction to the MEANING (positioning). Note who is speaking (role) — experts and visitors value different things.

## Layer 1 — operational (fact + quote + line)
- **P1 Associations with the current brand** [list+quote] — what the current name/image is compared to (e.g. "Beryozka"), tone (neutral/ironic/negative).
- **P2 What the name conveys vs hides** [open-ended] — is the essence readable; what gets lost; clunkiness/memorability.
- **P3 Name options** [list+quote] — specific suggestions; what they like/reject and why.
- **P4 References** [list] — which museums/brands they look to (Paris, Vienna MAK, V&A…), what they value in them.
- **P5 Identity and visuals** [open-ended] — logo, brand style, signage, website, social media — what they mention.
- **P6 Recognition and audience** [open-ended] — the recognition problem; who the brand's target audience is; the gap between "who we are / how we're seen".
- **P7 Renaming process** [open-ended] — how it should be changed: experts → employees → public vote; risks of the change.

## Layer 2 — analytical (conclusion + quote; CAPS) — to council
- **A1 Semantic core of the brand** — what must not be lost in any change (the essence, not the word). *(unstable)*
- **A2 Gap between current ↔ desired positioning** — where they want to shift and why.
- **A3 Renaming risk** — HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW (loss of recognition, resistance from old-timers, legal/political context).
- **A4 Fault line of opinions** — where experts vs employees vs visitors diverge on the brand (this is a TENSION for synthesis).
- **A5 Key insight** — 1 sentence: the brand is perceived as [X], but [gap/risk], therefore [recommendation on naming/positioning].

## Forcing questions
What does the name make you think of? What does it NOT convey about the museum? What options come to mind? Which museum would you want to resemble? What would we lose if we renamed it? Who would be against it?
