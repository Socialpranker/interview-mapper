# Lens: Win/Loss Interview

**When:** interview with a customer AFTER the purchase decision has already been made (bought from us / bought from a competitor / walked away from all options). Difference from CustDev: there the problem is still being validated, here the outcome is already known — the focus is on WHAT exactly tipped the scale and what it was compared against.
**Coding unit:** a moment of comparing alternatives with a direct quote + which alternative it refers to.
**Collection discipline:** keep an explicit axis "us vs alternative X vs status quo/inaction" in every cell. Don't let the respondent generalize — ask for a specific comparison episode.

## Layer 1 — operational (facts + quote + line)
- **W1 Outcome** — bought from us / bought from a specific competitor (name it) / walked away from all / postponed.
- **W2 List of alternatives considered** — who/what else they compared, including "doing nothing".
- **W3 Comparison criteria** — which parameters they compared on (price, features, support, reputation, referral) — in the customer's own words.
- **W4 Decisive moment** — specific episode/conversation/demo that tipped the choice one way or the other.
- **W5 Roles in the decision** — who else took part in the choice (decision maker, user, procurement), whose opinion prevailed.
- **W6 Sales/presentation process** — how the engagement went with us vs the alternative (who responded faster, who understood the need).
- **W7 Price gap** — how far apart the prices were; willingness to pay more/save, and for exactly what.

## Layer 2 — analytical (conclusion + quote; CAPS) — to council
- **A1 Key differentiator** — what actually decided the outcome (not what the customer names first out of politeness). *(unstable)*
- **A2 Reason category** — PRODUCT / PRICE / RELATIONSHIP-TRUST / TIMING / CUSTOMER'S INTERNAL POLITICS.
- **A3 Weak spot in the sales process** — where we (or the competitor) lost/gained points in the process itself, not in the product.
- **A4 Reversibility of the outcome** — if lost — is there a chance to win the customer back and under what condition; if won — risk of later switching to a competitor.
- **A5 Credibility of the explanation** — how much the stated reason resembles a real motive vs a polite/socially acceptable explanation (see Mom Test). *(unstable)*
- **A6 Key insight** — 1 sentence: outcome [X] was determined by factor [Y] at moment [Z]; for sales/product this means [consequence].

## Forcing questions
Who else did you consider? What happened at the moment the scale tipped? If [competitor/we] had had [Y], would the decision have been different? Who had the final say? What could change everything now?
