# Lens: Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)

**When:** customer/user interview to understand the "job" the person "hires" the product for, and the context of the choice. Not for an employee interview about org structure.
**Coding unit:** a statement about situation/motivation/outcome, backed by a direct quote about PAST behavior (not a hypothesis).
**Collection discipline:** facts about the past are valuable, not opinions about the future (see The Mom Test).

## Layer 1 — operational (facts + quote + line)
- **J1 Main job (job statement)** — "when [situation], I want [motivation], so that [outcome]". Verb + object + context.
- **J2 Trigger / struggling moment** — what made them look for a solution right then (event, deadline, breakdown).
- **J3 Job map** — step by step: define/plan → locate → prepare → confirm → execute → monitor → adjust → conclude. Where it stalls.
- **J4 Current solution and workarounds** — what they use now, what they "hired" before, what they dropped and why (specifically).
- **J5 Desired outcomes** — measurable success criteria in the customer's words ("do X faster", "fewer errors in Y").
- **J6 Related and emotional jobs** — what else gets solved along the way; the social/emotional layer ("so I don't look incompetent").

## Layer 2 — analytical (conclusion + quote; CAPS keyword) — to council
- **A1 Forces of progress** — PUSH (pain of the current) / PULL (draw of the new) / HABIT / ANXIETY. For each — a quote. *(unstable)*
- **A2 Key unmet outcome** — where the gap between importance and satisfaction is greatest.
- **A3 "Hiring/firing" criterion** — what actually decided the choice (not what the customer declares).
- **A4 Job-based segment** — which job-segment they belong to (not demographics, but job + context).
- **A5 Adoption barrier** — what prevents switching right now.
- **A6 Key insight** — 1 sentence: the customer "hires" X for [job], but [force/barrier], therefore [implication for the product].

## Forcing questions
Tell me about the last time you ran into this — what exactly did you do? What did you try before that? What happened on the day you decided to look for a solution? How much time/money have you already spent? Who else does this bother?
