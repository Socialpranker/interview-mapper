# Lens: Expert Interview

**When:** interview with an expert/council member to gather judgments, disagreements, and recommendations in a subject area. Not about the expert's personal role, but about their knowledge of the field.
**Coding unit:** an expert's claim with a quote + a note on the degree of confidence and status (fact / assessment / forecast).

## Layer 1 — operational (claims + quote + line)
- **E1 Expertise profile** — what exactly they are competent in, relevant experience, limits of competence (what they consider outside it).
- **E2 Key claims** — the main statements on the topic, each with a quote; note the type (fact / assessment / forecast).
- **E3 Recommendations** — specific proposals for action, priority.
- **E4 Data and precedents** — what they rely on: numbers, cases, sources, analogues.
- **E5 Constraints and risks** — what they warn about, where they see dangers/pitfalls.
- **E6 Open questions** — what they don't know themselves / where additional verification is needed.

## Layer 2 — analytical (conclusion + quote; CAPS) — to council
- **A1 Claim confidence** — HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW for each key claim (based on grounding in data vs opinion). *(unstable)*
- **A2 Points of consensus** — where it aligns with the mainstream/other experts (if known).
- **A3 Points of disagreement** — where it diverges, what exactly the dispute is about, whose position is better argued. *(unstable)*
- **A4 Contested/unverifiable statements** — what cannot be taken on faith and needs verification.
- **A5 Value for the decision** — how the claims affect the original decision/strategy.
- **A6 Key insight** — 1 sentence: the expert claims [X] with confidence [Y], which for the decision means [Z].

## Forcing questions
What is this conclusion based on — data or experience? Where could you be wrong? Which of your colleagues would disagree with you and why? What would change your mind? What is contested here?
