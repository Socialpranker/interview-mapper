# Lens: Exit Interview (departing employee)

**When:** interview with an employee who has already decided to leave or is resigning. Difference from org-mapping VMDI: the respondent is outside the consequence zone, honesty is usually higher, focus is on the reason for leaving and the point of no return, not the general system layout.
**Coding unit:** a statement about the reason for/episode of leaving with a direct quote; separately — what could have retained them (retention signal).
**Collection discipline:** look for the MOMENT the decision matured, and the SPECIFIC trigger, not a generic "it built up." Distinguish the declared reason (in the resignation letter/to HR) from the real one.

## Layer 1 — operational (facts + quote + line)
- **X1 Official vs real reason** — what is stated formally; what they actually say (often diverges).
- **X2 Point of no return** — specific episode/period after which the decision became irreversible.
- **X3 Trigger event** — the last straw: what happened right before the decision to look for/accept an offer.
- **X4 Search timeline** — when they started looking at options; in parallel with work or after deciding to leave.
- **X5 Where they're going and why there** — new place/reason (money / growth / conditions / people / other); what's there that wasn't here.
- **X6 What held them back until the end** — people, projects, habit, fear of change — what outweighed for a long time.
- **X7 Feedback to management during tenure** — did they raise the issue before, to whom, what was the response/action.

## Layer 2 — analytical (conclusion + quote; CAPS) — to council
- **A1 Reason category** — COMPENSATION / GROWTH / MANAGEMENT / RELATIONSHIPS / BURNOUT / EXTERNAL (relocation, etc.) / MIXED. *(unstable)*
- **A2 Preventability** — PREVENTABLE (the system could have reacted earlier) / NOT PREVENTABLE. Basis — whether management knew in advance (see X7).
- **A3 Retention signal** — what specifically could have retained them (not "more money" in general, but a specific condition/moment).
- **A4 Systemic pattern vs individual case** — does this resemble other people's reasons for leaving (if known) or is it a unique case. *(unstable)*
- **A5 Reputational echo risk** — what they'll tell former colleagues/the market; tone (neutral/negative/loyal).
- **A6 Key insight** — 1 sentence: employee is leaving because of [real reason], the decision became irreversible at [X], preventability — [Y].

## Forcing questions
When did you realize you'd definitely leave — what exactly happened that day/week? What would have changed the decision a month earlier? Who did you tell about the problem before, and what did you hear back? What does the new place have that this one doesn't? What will you miss, and what won't you?
