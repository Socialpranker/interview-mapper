three weeks ago on a Saturday.

## Layer 2 — Analytical

- **A1 Visitor eNPS** *(unstable cell)* — closer to NEUTRAL with a lean toward promoter driven by
  one strong element: she specifically recommends the mirror room, but clearly warns acquaintances
  about the limitations ("I'll warn them that some halls cost extra," line 94), meaning it's not
  an unconditional recommendation of the whole museum. What would shift it toward promoter —
  fixing the planetarium failure and adding a cafe (see V8, line 100: "If they fixed the
  planetarium and had a cafe, I'd come back a lot more willingly").
  Support: "Overall, yes, because of the mirror room, it was worth it for that alone" (line 92).

- **A2 Emotional arc** — starts neutral-wary (doubts because of the website, line 10), friction at
  the entrance/ticket counter (lines 16—22), a dip in the first hall (child's boredom, line 28), a
  sharp peak in the mirror room (line 36), a small dip from the unexpected extra charge (line 46),
  a steady middle stretch (maze — positive, illusions — neutral), a final dip in the
  mini-planetarium (line 62), and logistical friction on the way out (restroom, snack bar, gift
  stand).
  Peak support: "That was just wow" (line 36); dip support: "That's where it really fell apart,
  honestly, the projector was flickering, the picture was jittering, the sound was crackling"
  (line 62).

- **A3 Moment of truth** — the mirror room outweighed the rest of the visit and determined the
  final rating and intent to return, despite the planetarium failure and logistical friction.
  Support: "It's like a good song with terrible sound at the end, you know, the mirror room is
  just gorgeous, but toward the finale everything fell apart into disappointment with that
  planetarium and no food" (line 90).

- **A4 Key barrier** — operational (fixable): a broken projector in the planetarium, no
  cafe/vending machine, no online ticket sales, and no signs to the restroom — all fixable without
  changing the building or the location. The structural barrier (the museum's location in a
  courtyard behind a market) is secondary and explicitly described as fixable through navigation
  and signage rather than relocation.
  Support: "the museum turned out to be kind of out of the way, behind a market, the GPS glitched
  twice" (line 16, structural, but mitigable) vs. "the projector was flickering, the picture was
  jittering, the sound was crackling, it felt like the equipment was really old and nobody had
  fixed it in a long time" (line 62, operational, fixable via repair/replacement of the equipment).

- **A5 Visit metaphor** — "a song with terrible sound at the end": the visitor experiences the
  visit as a single piece of work with strong content, but the quality of the finale (the
  planetarium) spoils the aftertaste, even though the climax (the mirror room) came earlier in the
  route, not at the end.
  Support: "It's like a good song with terrible sound at the end" (line 90).

- **A6 Main insight** — The visitor (a family visit with a child, reached through a chance
  word-of-mouth video) came for a spectacular peak (the mirror room), but the route is structured
  so the visit physically ends in the weakest hall (the broken planetarium) and without any option
  to grab a bite or buy a souvenir, so the final recommendation ends up split ("this one — yes,
  the rest — not required") rather than wholly positive, and the return is postponed until the
  operational problems specifically (planetarium, cafe) are fixed, not the mirror room itself.
  Support: "I'll say the mirror room is a must-see... but you can skip the rest, especially I
  wouldn't recommend the planetarium" (line 94).

## Ambiguous spots (for unstable cells)

1. **A1 (eNPS)** — a split recommendation (praising one zone while warning about the rest) doesn't
   fit cleanly into the promoter/neutral/detractor category; the respondent gives no numeric
   score herself, so the conclusion is an interpretation with a confidence caveat.
2. **Reason for the maze's extra charge** — the respondent herself is unsure whether it's a
   mistake by the ticket counter/website or a deliberate museum practice: "maybe they just forgot
   to update the information on the website, or maybe they deliberately don't write it so a
   person already there doesn't turn around and leave, it's hard to say for sure" (line 106) — do
   not map as a fact about the museum's intent, leave it as an open question.
3. **The moment with the attendant singing along** (line 102) — emotionally meaningful to the
   visitor ("it made me feel so warm," "really human"), but the respondent herself explicitly
   believes the museum is unlikely to track such moments (line 104) — an example of a
   value-creating moment the organization doesn't notice, not to be confused with the official
   service of the hall.
