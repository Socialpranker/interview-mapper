48	## Layer 2 — analytical (CAPITALIZED conclusion + anchor quote)
49	
50	### A1 — Forces of progress *(unstable)*
51	**Conclusion:** PUSH — recurring errors in manual tracking (underpaying Kate plus a similar case with another barista) and the respondent's lost time (3-4 hours a week on reconciliation). PULL — the vivid "Shift+" demo with automatic calculation and geolocation check-in, shown live. ANXIETY — fear that older employees wouldn't master the app, and fear of a repeat mistake during the transition itself. HABIT — WhatsApp is simple and requires no training, the partner suggested tolerating the old setup for another six months.
51	**Anchor quote (push, final reassessment):** "After that he agreed we couldn't keep dragging it out, the risk of errors was more costly than the habit" (line 55)
52	**Additional anchor quote (habit):** "My partner even said, maybe forget it, let's stick with the old system for another six months, why break everything now" (line 52)
53	**Additional anchor quote (anxiety):** "Of course there was, I was afraid the baristas would refuse to bother with a new app, we've got people of different ages, some of them aren't into technology at all" (line 48)
54	**⚑ Why unstable:** the respondent himself gives an unverifiable quantitative estimate of the push/anxiety ratio ("forty percent doubt, sixty percent that we needed to act", line 75) and immediately qualifies it by noting that in hindsight there seems to have been less doubt (line 76) — the weight distribution between the forces requires the advice of independent runs (S3), it cannot be pinned down unambiguously.
