Source: `transcript.txt` (95 lines, diarized: Moderator + 5 participants — Dmitri, Olga, Igor, Svetlana, Artyom).
Topic: testing the "Flex" plan of the ready-meal delivery service "MealNearby".

## Layer 1 — operational

### G1 Group composition and roles
Fact: five participants with varying levels of category experience — from an active user (Dmitri) to a newcomer (Artyom); a clear opinion leader emerged from the first replies (Dmitri).
Quote: "Dmitri, 41, I've been using services like this for about three years, right now I'm on two at once, you could say I'm an advanced user of the category." — Dmitri, line 2.
Additionally (composition): "Artyom, 24, I've actually never used services like this at all, I cook myself or order from a cafe." — Artyom, line 6.

### G2 Topic and stimulus material
Fact: the group discusses a mockup of the subscription screen for the new "Flex" plan — with the ability to change the weekly meal set with no cancellation penalty.
Quote: "Today we're discussing a new subscription format from the ready-meal delivery service \"MealNearby\" — the \"Flex\" plan, where you can change the weekly meal set with no cancellation penalty." — Moderator, line 7.

### G3 Individual positions (first opinion before group influence)
- Dmitri (line 8): "the main pain point is the rigid weekly cart that you can't change after Tuesday... but honestly I don't believe it until I see the price." — skeptical about the price from the start.
- Olga (line 10): "I actually liked it visually, the light interface, and the fact that they show calories right on the dish card — that's convenient" — positive first impression, focused on visuals/calories.
- Igor (line 14): "I... honestly, I didn't fully get how you switch between weeks going forward." — first opinion: confusion, didn't understand the navigation.
- Svetlana (line 20): "My experience has been more negative with my current plan, actually, I switched to cooking at home last month specifically because of the cancellation penalties." — initially negative experience with penalties.
- Artyom (line 24): "I think it sounds convenient, I'm ready to try it, especially if it's really penalty-free." — initially positive, uncritical stance of a newcomer.

### G4 Agreement/disagreement (reaction map)
Thesis "a one-hour delivery window is more convenient than the previous three hours" (line 79) — for: Dmitri, Svetlana, Olga (lines 80–82); against: Igor (lines 84, 86); silent: Artyom.
Quote (for): "An hour is actually more convenient, I'd rather know the exact time than wait at home for three hours not knowing when, so I'm for narrowing the window." — Dmitri, line 80.
Quote (against): "for me it's actually the opposite — I work shifts, and a tight one-hour window is inconvenient for me, I'd rather wait longer" — Igor, line 84.

CONCLUSION: THE GROUP SPLITS ALONG THE LINE OF CATEGORY EXPERIENCE: EXPERIENCED USERS (DMITRI, SVETLANA) PERCEIVE THE PRICE AND TERMS AS INFLATED/SUSPICIOUS BECAUSE THEY HAVE A BASIS FOR COMPARISON, WHILE THE NEWCOMER (ARTYOM) PERCEIVES THE SAME PRICE AS NORMAL PRECISELY BECAUSE HE LACKS ONE — THIS DIVERGENCE IS EXPLICITLY VOICED BY THE GROUP ITSELF.

### A5 Method limitation
CONCLUSION: THIS GROUP'S CONCLUSIONS CANNOT BE GENERALIZED TO THE WHOLE AUDIENCE — N=1 GROUP OF 5 PEOPLE, A STRONG COMPOSITION EFFECT (ONE DOMINANT PARTICIPANT SKEWS THE DISCUSSION), AND ALSO A MODERATOR EFFECT, SINCE THE MODERATOR DIRECTLY DREW OUT THE OPINION OF THE SILENT PARTICIPANT (IGOR) — WITHOUT WHICH HIS POSITION MIGHT NEVER HAVE SURFACED AT ALL.
Anchor quote: "Igor, you've been quiet — what do you think about the first impression?" — Moderator, line 13.

### A6 Main insight
CONCLUSION: ON THE TOPIC OF THE "FLEX" PLAN, THE GROUP GIVES A MIXED PICTURE — THERE IS GENUINE CONSENSUS ON PACKAGING AND THE DELIVERY WINDOW (EXCEPT FOR IGOR), BUT ON PRICE AND CANCELLATION TERMS THE CONSENSUS IS MORE CONFORMIST, DRIVEN BY A SINGLE VOICE; THE KEY GENUINE DIVERGENCE IS BETWEEN EXPERIENCED AND NEW USERS IN HOW THEY PERCEIVE THE PRICE; GENERALIZE WITH CAUTION — THE GROUP IS SMALL AND HEAVILY INFLUENCED BY ONE PARTICIPANT.
